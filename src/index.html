An overview on low-level software engineering job roles

Introduction

The computer science job market is currently one of the most competitive at the moment, with thousands of applicants applying for a single position sometimes. Companies want people with a strong understanding of computer science and programming concepts. This is especially important in low-level roles, where it is crucial to have a deep understanding of systems architecture.

Overview of the Roles

1. Linux Kernel Engineer:

  • Develop and maintain Linux kernel modules.
  • Troubleshoot and debug kernel issues.
  • Collaborate with teams to optimize performance and security.
  • Create, troubleshoot and maintain linux kernel instances on multiple networks.

2. Systems Software Engineer:

  • Design and implement system-level software components.
  • Optimize operating system performance and resource management.
  • Develop and implement plans to ensure quality and performance.
  • Interact with hardware to ensure efficient software-hardware integration.

3. Embedded Systems Developer:

  • Develop and maintain software for embedded systems.
  • Work closely with hardware teams to define interfaces and requirements.
  • Conduct testing and debugging of embedded applications.
  • Ensure compliance with industry standards and performance benchmarks.
